To write down
the independent variables call the first two rows of $U$ $u_1$ and $u_2$ which are $k$-dimensional
complex vectors and they satisfy $\bar{u}_{alpha} u_{\beta} = delta_{alpha \beta}$ because $U$ was
unitary ($alpha, \beta = 1, 2$). Because in the $C_i$ the only nonvanishing components are given by
the upper-left $2 \times 2$ block from $U$ only the first two rows ($u_1$ and $u_2$) will enter
the expressions and remember that $e_i$ is an arbitrary unit vector and $e_0>1$ is also arbitrary. Let's transform everything
back to the $E_i$ and $E_0$ which were the normalized traceless parts of $\rho_i$ and $\rho_0=S$
(define $e_0=ch H$):
$$\frac{1}{lambda}left( \rho_i^{ab} - \frac{Tr\rho_i}{k} delta_{ab} \right) = left( U^{dagger} E_i U \right)_{ab} = \frac{1}{2} left( sh H (delta_{ij}-e_i e_j)+ ch H e_i e_j \right) \bar{u}_a \tau_i u_b
+ e_i left( \frac{\bar{u}_a u_b}{2} - \frac{delta_{ab}}{k}\right)$$
$$\frac{1}{lambda}left( S_{ab} - \frac{Tr S}{k} delta_{ab} \right) = left( U^{dagger} E_0 U \right)_{ab} = \frac{1}{2} e_i \bar{u}_a \tau_i u_b + ch H left( \frac{\bar{u}_a u_b}{2} - \frac{delta_{ab}}{k}\right)$$
The appearing $u_1$ and $u_2$ complex vectors resemble the original $u$ and $w$ that we defined from the
$zeta$'s but the $u_1$ and $u_2$ are normalized.
