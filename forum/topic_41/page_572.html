turkáltam és ilyet találtam róla:
"Everyone and their grandmother know about Kazaa; a few even know about Kazaa’s cleaner cousin “Kazaa Lite”. Hardly anyone I have spoken to has heard of Direct Connect ++, also called DC++. This tool can be way better than Kazaa or worse depending on how much stuff you share. If you are a LEECH (someone who does not share, and only downloads), you will find that this tool is useless.
WHY you may ask:
well, DC++ unlike Kazaa is very fragmented. One connects to servers that have their own set of users. Servers do not share users between each other. This makes DC++ both way better and way worse, depending on which server you connect to. Additionally, most large communities or servers require that you share in excess of 50 GB. That’s more than the full hard drive capacity of some computers.
Advantages of this approach are plentiful: one can start a community of like minded users and decide what the entry criteria are; almost like exclusive clubs. Additionally, the download speeds are in excess of 50 Kbps for files that are shared by multiple users; assuming that you yourself are on a broadband connection.
For the select few who have more than 50 GB to share, there is a wealth of information that can be downloaded at super fast speeds. Additionally, the files one gets on DC++ are typically of better quality that those on Kazaa, simply because many Kazaa users use dummy files to increase their ratings."
(forrás: http://www.ranjeetsodhi.com/archives/000041.html)
